InfoNCE Induces Gaussian Distribution

ICLR 2026

Roy Betser*, Eyal Gofer*, Meir Yossef Levi, Guy Gilboa
Technion — Israel Institute of Technology
*Equal contribution
Paper (PDF) Code (coming soon) arXiv

Abstract

Contrastive learning has become a cornerstone of modern representation learning. We show that the population InfoNCE objective induces asymptotically Gaussian structure in learned representations. Our analysis proceeds along two complementary routes: an empirical idealization route based on alignment saturation and thin-shell concentration, and a regularized route requiring milder assumptions. In both cases, finite-dimensional projections converge to multivariate Gaussian distributions as representation dimension grows.

We support the theory with experiments on synthetic data, CIFAR-10, and pretrained foundation models, demonstrating norm concentration and coordinate-wise Gaussianity across architectures and settings. These results provide a principled explanation for the widely observed Gaussian structure of contrastive representations.

Main Contributions

Bounded Alignment. Alignment induced by InfoNCE is fundamentally constrained by augmentation mildness via an HGR-based bound.

Uniformity on the Sphere. Along both analytical routes, normalized representations converge toward spherical uniformity.

Asymptotic Gaussian Structure. Both normalized and unnormalized representations exhibit Gaussian finite-dimensional projections.

Empirical Validation. Finite-dimensional experiments confirm the predicted Gaussian behavior across synthetic and real datasets.
